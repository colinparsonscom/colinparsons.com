A bit about me

I’m a rising junior at WashU majoring in math, philosophy, neuroscience, and psychology, and minoring in Spanish. I've always been passionate about math, so I started taking math courses online from Stanford around my sophomore year of high school. As a result of this focus, I got connected in high school with a program called GravityGames, where I led the team making the first-ever game to be 3D printed on the International Space Station via MadeInSpace's 3D printer! That game was called StarCatcher, and I'm now, in partnership with SEDS, I'm expanding GravityGames into a nationwide program so that college students across the country can make games on the ISS. Through GravityGames, I got connected with a VR company in the Bay Area and started manufacturing my own virtual reality viewers. After showing my work to the team, I joined the data analysis group at Eyefluence just before the end of my time in high school. I then came to college, and we were acquired by Google about two months into my time at WashU! At Eyefluence, I was really impressed by a machine learning technique called neural networks. I taught myself a bit about these, then worked in computer vision and machine learning research at an enterprise security company called Centrify. I've worked for a little while in asset diversification at WashU's endowment. I was also approached by Eyefluence’s former CEO about joining his AI startup Rival Theory! I worked both on the technical side, considering models for basic concept learning and consciousness, as well as on the business side, in strategy. Now, I lead a 4-person team at WashU for a venture capital firm called Contrary, investing pre-seed capital in companies started by students, professors, and recent graduates. I’m managing our first-ever “Summer in SF” accelerator. Besides helping out our portfolio companies this summer, I’m building a machine learning system with a friend that we hope will offer some significant progress in hierarchical concept learning and generalization! This project will likely become my main focus during my remaining two years at WashU.
